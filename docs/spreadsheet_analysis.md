# NAV Spreadsheet Analysis

**File**: VerisCapitalAMC_NAV_20260316_working.xlsx (6.3 MB, 70+ sheets)

## Summary

The spreadsheet is the current working NAV workbook for Veris Capital AMC, containing position data from July 2025 through March 2026. It combines three layers of data collection: Power Queries that pull on-chain data at specific blocks, manual calculation sheets for positions that require accrual-based valuation, and periodic portfolio snapshots imported from third-party tools (1Token, DeBank, ForDefi).

The workbook is structured around two core sheets. The NAV Summary sheet (sheet "1. NAV Summary") serves as the master position list, with one row per position and historical valuations across approximately 18 dates. Assets and liabilities are separated -- leveraged positions appear as two distinct rows, one for the collateral (asset) and one for the debt (liability). Each position carries metadata including token ticker, type (Direct Lending, DeFi Product, Structured Product), legal contract status, investment date, and columns for valuation method, default valuation source, and validation source. The Valuation sheet (sheet "2. Valuation") provides a more granular per-date breakdown with buy/sell/balance tracking and verification columns that compare values from multiple sources (1Token, DeBank, Solana explorers, protocol reports, and manual entries).

The spreadsheet already automates nine on-chain queries via Power Queries. These query Chainlink for the mF-ONE oracle price, read mF-ONE token balances, pull Morpho position data (collateral and borrow) for two markets (mF-ONE/USDC and AA_FalconXUSDC/USDC), read the CreditCoop vault value via convertToAssets, fetch the Pareto FalconX tranche price, compute the Gauntlet FalconX net exposure and Veris's pro-rata share, read Fluid vault positions on Plasma via the vault resolver contract, and batch-fetch token prices from CoinGecko Pro. Each query logs results with a tag, contract address, wallet, block number, and block timestamp in UTC, creating an audit trail. The historical versions of these queries (tbl_h_q_* sheets) append results over time, while the live versions (q_* sheets) show only the latest snapshot.

However, a significant portion of the portfolio is not covered by these queries. All Solana positions are handled manually -- this includes Kamino Solstice obligations (USCC collateral with USDC debt, PT-USX with USX debt, syrupUSDC), Exponent Finance positions (PT-ONyc-13MAY26 LP, PT-USX-01JUN26, eUSX), and OnRe (ONyc). On the EVM side, the Aave Horizon USCC/RLUSD leveraged position, additional Morpho markets on Arbitrum (syrupUSDC/USDT, syrupUSDC/PYUSD, syrupUSDC/AUSD, syrupUSDC/RLUSD), the Avantis tranche on Base, the Hyperliquid USDC position on HyperEVM, and Strata/Ethena positions are all tracked through imported 1Token snapshots or manual entries rather than direct on-chain queries.

Four positions use manual accrual calculations in dedicated sheets rather than market or on-chain pricing. The FalconX/M11 position (Falcon Calc sheet) is the most complex: Veris holds approximately 9.376% of the Gauntlet vault, which itself holds a Morpho leveraged position in AA_FalconXUSDC. The accrual uses a contractual interest rate that has changed five times (from 11.25% in August 2025 down to 10% from February 2026). The on-chain Gauntlet vault value and Pareto tranche price serve as cross-references but not as primary valuation. The Credit Coop/Rain position (CCoop Calc sheet) tracks a $3.75M principal at 14% with a 10% performance fee, cross-referenced against the on-chain convertToAssets value (currently showing $3,855,095). The Giza Credit position (Giza Calc sheet) accrues at 16% on a $1.5M principal, with separate tracking for GIZA token incentive distributions that compensate for any shortfall versus the target return. The Resolv USR Yield Maxi position (Resolv Calc sheet) started at $2.5M with a partial withdrawal of approximately $1.5M in February 2026, accruing at a target rate that moved from 18% to 15%.

PT tokens are tracked in the PT Calc sheet with individual lot amortisation. Each purchase is recorded separately with its transaction date, token quantity, purchase value, gain to maturity, and maturity date. The sheet then computes yield-to-date and valuation at each reporting date using linear amortisation. Historical PT positions that have matured (PT-mHYPER-20NOV2025, PT-ONyc-25JAN26) remain in the sheet for record-keeping.

The Contract Address Book sheet provides several addresses not already in CLAUDE.md, most notably the two Morpho market IDs (mF-ONE/USDC: 0xef2c308b5abecf5c8750a1aa82b47c558005feb7a03f4f8e1ad682d71ac8d0ba and AA_FalconXUSDC/USDC: 0xe83d72fa5b00dcd46d9e0e860d95aa540d5ec106da5833108a9f826f21f36f52), the Fluid Vault Resolver on Plasma (0x5471195328cB443c85097A7A7fF0A74eaB3Cb497), the Kamino obligation (HMMc5d9sMrGrAY18wE5yYTPpJNk72nrBrgqz5mtE3yrq) and market (9Y7uwXgQ68mGqRtZfuFaP4hc4fxeJ7cE9zTtqTxVhfGU) addresses on Solana, and specific token contract addresses for USDC, wstUSR, and USDT0. The CoinGecko helper table maps approximately 20 token IDs for batch price fetching.

The Manual entries sheet contains wallet-level token balances across all chains -- small USDC amounts, governance tokens (MORPHO, PENDLE, ARB), native gas tokens (ETH, AVAX, XPL), and other residual balances. This is functionally identical to what our collect_balances.py script already produces.

Several positions visible in the historical columns have since been exited: Gauntlet USDC Frontier, PT-USDe and PT-sUSDe (matured September 2025), Smokehouse USDC, Jupiter lend, sUSDe, Gauntlet USDC Core, MEV Capital USDC, Makina DUSD, USDC Fluid lending, and various Hyperithm positions. The mF-ONE vault balance went to zero by 23 March 2026 and the Morpho AA_FalconXUSDC position was closed on 16 March 2026, suggesting active portfolio restructuring.

The largest current positions by value are the Fluid wstUSR/USDT0 leveraged position on Plasma (approximately $22.1M collateral against $22.5M debt), the Morpho syrupUSDC/USDT position on Arbitrum (approximately $13.4M collateral against $10.8M debt), the Kamino USCC/USDC Solstice position on Solana (approximately $8.5M collateral against $6.8M debt), the mF-ONE position on Ethereum (approximately $4.1M valued via oracle at $1.068/token, held as Morpho collateral with $1.3M USDC debt), the Credit Coop vault (approximately $3.8M), and the FalconX/Gauntlet indirect exposure (approximately $2.7M as Veris's 9.4% share of the vault's net exposure).

For our Python script, the key takeaway is that the spreadsheet's Power Query pattern -- querying at a specific block, logging the block number and timestamp, and storing both raw and human-readable values -- is exactly the audit trail format that Bank Frick requires. Our script should replicate this pattern for every position, extending coverage to the Solana positions, additional EVM protocols, and A3 accrual calculations that the spreadsheet currently handles manually or through imported snapshots.
